Must-See Wonders

Sri Lanka's Most Famous
Attractions

From ancient rock citadels to whispering tea fields, thundering waterfalls and whale-watched shores — one island, infinite wonders.

UNESCO ★★★★★ Central Province

Sigiriya Rock Fortress

The legendary 5th-century sky palace rising 200m above the jungle — frescoes, water gardens and panoramic views that will leave you breathless.

UNESCO ★★★★★ Kandy

Temple of the Sacred Tooth

Sri Lanka's holiest Buddhist relic, set within a moat-ringed palace complex in lush hill-country Kandy.

UNESCO ★★★★★ Southern Province

Galle Fort

A beautifully preserved Dutch colonial walled city with cobblestone streets, boutique galleries and stunning ocean ramparts.

National Park ★★★★★ Southern Province

Yala National Park

World's highest leopard density, plus elephants, crocodiles, peacocks — Africa-style safari on a tropical island.

Highlands ★★★★★ Uva Province

Ella & Nine Arch Bridge

Emerald rice paddies, the iconic colonial viaduct, train rides through mist and hikes up Little Adam's Peak.

UNESCO ★★★★★ North Central Province

Anuradhapura — Ancient Capital

Towering white dagobas, the sacred Bodhi Tree (oldest documented tree on Earth) and 2,500 years of continuous civilisation.

Coastal ★★★★★ Southern Province

Mirissa & Whale Watching

A golden crescent beach famous for blue whale encounters (Dec–Apr) and unforgettable sunset cocktails.

Tea Country ★★★★★ Central Province

Nuwara Eliya — Little England

Cool colonial hill station surrounded by rolling tea estates, misty waterfalls and English-style gardens at 1,868m altitude.

UNESCO ★★★★★ North Central Province

Polonnaruwa Ruins

The medieval capital's royal palaces, colossal Buddha statues and lotus-filled moats — cycling through ancient grandeur.

Pilgrimage ★★★★★ Sabaragamuwa Province

Adam's Peak — Sri Pada

A pre-dawn pilgrimage to a 2,243m sacred summit — the triangular shadow at sunrise is one of earth's most magical moments.

Wildlife ★★★★★ North Central Province

Minneriya — The Gathering

The world's largest wild elephant gathering (Aug–Oct) — hundreds of elephants congregate at an ancient reservoir in one breathtaking spectacle.

UNESCO ★★★★★ Central Province

Dambulla Cave Temple

Five dazzling rock-cave sanctuaries with 150 Buddha statues and vivid ceiling frescoes spanning 2,000 years of devotional art.

Coastal ★★★★★ Eastern Province

Trincomalee & Nilaveli

Pristine white-sand beaches, crystal-clear snorkelling reefs and the ancient Koneswaram temple perched on Swami Rock.
